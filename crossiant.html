Receipe for Crossiants.

A croissant is a flaky and buttery pastry of Austrian origin, known for its crescent shape. This popular French breakfast item is made from layered yeast-leavened dough, which is folded and rolled multiple times to create a laminated texture. The result is a delicate, golden-brown exterior with a tender and airy interior. Croissants are often enjoyed on their own or filled with various ingredients such as chocolate, almond paste, or ham and cheese. The distinctive layers and rich flavor make croissants a beloved treat worldwide.



Incredients List:

  1. tablespoons warm water (110 degrees F/45 degrees C)
  2. 1 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  3. 1 teaspoon white sugar
  4. 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  5. ⅔ cup warm milk
  6. 2 teaspoons white sugar
  7. 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  8. 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  9. ⅔ cup unsalted butter, chilled
  10. 1 large egg
  11. 1 tablespoon water

Step by step Receipe:

make easy dough from butter, flour, sugar, salt, yeast, and milk

roll out dough into a large rectangle

make the butter layer

enclose the butter layer inside the dough

roll out the dough into another large rectangle, then fold it back together

roll out the dough again, fold it back together again

roll out the dough one more time, fold it back together

shape the croissants

bake
